What We Cover — Start to Finish

Damage Identification & Documentation

We identify and document the full extent of damage — char depth, smoke migration, compromised systems — and direct findings to the correct licensed professionals, including structural engineers when structural integrity is in question. That work belongs to a PE. Finding it belongs to us.

Smoke & Odor Migration

Smoke travels well beyond the fire's origin. We map migration into unburned rooms, HVAC systems, attic spaces, and wall cavities — every affected area documented.

Emergency Stabilization

Board-up, tarping, site security, and temporary utility coordination in the critical first hours — protecting the structure and locking in claim value before anything is disturbed.

IICRC Loss Classification

We classify the full scope of loss to IICRC standards. Classification determines which specialists are needed, in what sequence, and what the insurance carrier is obligated to cover.

Industrial Hygienist Planning

We develop a mitigation plan in coordination with a certified industrial hygienist — a step most contractors skip, and the one that determines whether the remediation is done correctly.

Expert Network Coordination

We are not the people the insurance company recommends. We bring in the experts each situation actually requires — structural engineers, hygienists, remediation specialists — coordinated by Jason so the homeowner doesn't have to manage a dozen different contractors. Every expert is there because the damage demands it, not because a carrier approved it.

Secondary Specialty

Catastrophic
Water Damage

Water losses are serious. Even when they don't look dramatic, a significant water event can affect structure, air quality, and long-term habitability in ways that take months to appear. We work best alongside homeowners whose loss involves multiple systems or areas of the home — not because we turn anyone away, but because our level of involvement is built for events that require it. If you're not sure whether your situation qualifies, call us. We'll give you an honest answer and, if we're not the right fit, point you to someone who is.

IICRC Classification First

Category 1, 2, or 3 — we classify the loss to IICRC standards before any work begins. Classification determines which specialists enter the property and in what order.

Industrial Hygienist Mitigation Plan

We develop a written mitigation plan in coordination with a certified industrial hygienist — defining scope, safety protocols, and remediation standards before a single tool is picked up.

Coordinated Specialist Network

We refer drying, mold remediation, and reconstruction to vetted preferred-network specialists — the right people for each phase, coordinated and overseen by Jason throughout.

When you have a house fire or major water loss, your insurance company will likely recommend contractors. Those contractors work within what the carrier is willing to pay — and they tend to see the damage the same way the adjuster does. That is not an accident.

We are not those contractors. We are not on any carrier's preferred vendor list. We identify damage as it actually exists, document everything, and bring in the experts the situation requires — not the ones a carrier has pre-approved. Our goal is straightforward: the home is returned to its pre-loss condition. Every decision is made in the homeowner's interest. Period.

Jason personally manages every project we accept. We work with a small number of homeowners each year because each one deserves that level of involvement — not a handoff to a crew that doesn't know their story.

Immediate Response & Technical Assessment

Jason arrives personally. He performs a full structural, smoke-migration, and hazmat assessment before the insurance adjuster documents anything. The first assessment frames the entire claim — being there first matters.

IICRC Classification & Scope Definition

We classify the loss to IICRC standards — fire category, water category, affected systems, and air quality risk. Classification determines which specialists the situation requires and what the carrier is obligated to fund.

A written mitigation plan is developed in coordination with a certified industrial hygienist. This plan defines what gets done, by whom, in what sequence, and to what standard — before any specialist sets foot on site.

Jason brings in the right specialists from our preferred network for each phase of work. He coordinates the sequence, monitors quality and safety, and stays the single point of accountability throughout — the homeowner never gets handed off.
